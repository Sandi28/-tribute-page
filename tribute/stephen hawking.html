STEPHEN HAWKING

1942-2018

Stephen Hawking, Theoretical Physicist, Cosmologist, Late Director of Research, Centre for Theoretical Cosmology, University of Cambridge



EARLY LIFE

Stephen Hawking was born on January 8, 1942, in Oxford, England. He attended St. Albans School and showed early academic brilliance in mathematics and physics. He studied physics at the University of Oxford and later pursued his graduate studies at the University of Cambridge, where he was diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) in 1963. Despite his physical limitations, Hawking continued his groundbreaking work in theoretical physics and cosmology, becoming one of the most renowned scientists of his time. His early life and struggles with ALS were documented in his autobiography, "A Brief History of Time.



CARRER

Stephen Hawking was a renowned theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and writer. His study on Hawking radiation, in particular, made a substantial addition to our understanding of black holes and the structure of the cosmos. At the University of Cambridge, Hawking spent more than three decades as the Lucasian Professor of Mathematics. In spite of his struggle with ALS, which left him nearly entirely paralyzed, he carried out ground-breaking research and wrote popular science books that made difficult scientific ideas understandable to a broad audience. His contributions and perseverance have forever changed the landscape of physics and scientific communication.

AWARDS AND HONORS

The Albert Einstein Prize for Outstanding Contributions to Theoretical Physics (1978). One of the greatest awards for scientific achievement is the Copley Medal (2006) from the Royal Society. The highest civilian award in the United States, the Presidential Medal of Freedom, was presented in 2009. Fellowships in prestigious associations such as the Royal Society, the Pontifical Academy of Sciences, and the American National Academy of Sciences. In recognition of his significant contributions to the fields of theoretical physics and cosmology, he received honorary degrees from several colleges all around the world.
